# Social Media Checklist for College Students
### Responsible Use Guide — Digital Literacy Portfolio | Task 4

---

## ✅ DO's

| # | Do This | Why It Matters |
|---|---------|----------------|
| 1 | **Keep your profiles professional** — use a clear photo, your real name, and a consistent bio across platforms. | Employers and internship coordinators routinely search your name before interviews. First impressions happen online. |
| 2 | **Think before you post** — ask yourself: *"Would I be comfortable if my professor, parent, or future employer saw this?"* | Content you post today can resurface years later. A 10-second pause can prevent long-term reputational damage. |
| 3 | **Use privacy settings actively** — review who can see your posts, stories, and tagged photos on each platform. | Default settings on most platforms are public. Controlling your audience protects you from unwanted attention and data misuse. |
| 4 | **Credit sources and creators** — always tag or mention the original author when sharing someone else's work, quote, or image. | Sharing without attribution is a form of plagiarism online. It also builds trust and goodwill with your network. |
| 5 | **Engage constructively** — comment, share, and discuss with respect, even when you disagree with someone's opinion. | Respectful disagreement builds your credibility. Thoughtful engagement also helps you build a genuine professional network. |
| 6 | **Keep personal and academic accounts separate** — maintain a professional LinkedIn/GitHub distinct from casual personal accounts. | Mixing the two can confuse your professional audience and make it harder to manage your image intentionally. |
| 7 | **Report harmful or abusive content** — use platform tools to flag misinformation, harassment, or inappropriate material. | You have a responsibility as a digital citizen. Reporting helps keep online spaces safer for everyone. |

---

## ❌ DON'Ts

| # | Avoid This | Why It's Harmful |
|---|------------|------------------|
| 1 | **Don't share personal information publicly** — avoid posting your phone number, home address, college ID, daily routine, or location in real time. | This information can be exploited by bad actors for identity theft, stalking, or targeted scams. |
| 2 | **Don't spread unverified information** — never share news, statistics, or claims without checking a credible source first. | Misinformation spreads faster than corrections. Sharing false content — even unknowingly — damages your credibility. |
| 3 | **Don't engage in or tolerate cyberbullying** — avoid posting, liking, or sharing content that mocks, demeans, or targets an individual. | Cyberbullying has serious psychological consequences for victims. Passive participation (liking/sharing) is still participation. |
| 4 | **Don't post during academic hours or exams** — avoid distraction-driven scrolling or posting when you should be in class or studying. | Chronic social media distraction is directly linked to lower academic performance and reduced attention span. |
| 5 | **Don't vent about college, faculty, or classmates publicly** — frustrations expressed online can damage relationships and your academic record. | Screenshots are permanent. What feels like harmless venting can reach the people you are talking about within minutes. |
| 6 | **Don't accept connection requests from strangers without verifying** — especially on LinkedIn, where fake recruiter profiles are common. | Fake profiles are used to harvest data, send phishing links, or conduct social engineering attacks on students. |
| 7 | **Don't plagiarise or misrepresent yourself** — don't claim skills, awards, or experiences on your profile that you do not have. | Academic and professional misrepresentation can lead to disqualification, expulsion, or termination if discovered. |

## Quick Reference Card

```
BEFORE YOU POST — ask yourself:
  [ ] Is this accurate and verified?
  [ ] Could this embarrass me professionally?
  [ ] Does this reveal private information?
  [ ] Am I crediting the original creator?
  [ ] Would I say this to someone's face?

If any answer is NO or UNSURE — don't post it.
```
